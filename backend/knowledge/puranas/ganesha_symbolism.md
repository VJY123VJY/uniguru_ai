## Content

Lord Ganesha (Ganapati, Vinayaka) is revered as the remover of obstacles (Vighnaharta) and the lord of beginnings. His distinctive form encodes spiritual symbolism rather than mere mythological description.

**Elephant Head:** The elephant head represents supreme wisdom (buddhi), discrimination, and memory. It signifies the ability to overcome obstacles with intelligence and patience.

**Large Ears:** Large ears symbolize attentive listening — the capacity to hear prayers, scriptures, and the needs of devotees.

**Small Eyes:** Small eyes indicate concentration and the ability to perceive the subtle essence of reality beyond surface appearance.

**Trunk:** The flexible trunk represents adaptability (viveka) — the power to discriminate between the real and unreal, and to grasp both the gross and subtle.

**Single Tusk (Ekdanta):** One tusk denotes one-pointed focus on the ultimate truth, transcending duality.

**Large Belly:** The generous belly symbolizes the capacity to digest all experiences of life — joy and sorrow — and to contain the cosmos within consciousness.

**Modaka (Sweet):** The sweet held in his hand represents the rewards of spiritual discipline and the bliss of Self-knowledge.

**Mouse (Mushika) as Vehicle:** The mouse represents desire and the restless mind. Ganesha's mastery over the mouse signifies control over desires and the ego.

**Four Arms:** His four arms represent the four inner instruments (antahkarana): mind (manas), intellect (buddhi), ego (ahankara), and consciousness (chitta).

**Broken Tusk and Writing the Mahabharata:** Tradition holds that Ganesha broke his tusk to write the Mahabharata when his pen failed, symbolizing sacrifice in the service of sacred knowledge (jnana).

In worship and meditation, Ganesha is invoked at the start of rituals because he clears inner and outer obstacles, granting clarity before any sacred undertaking.
